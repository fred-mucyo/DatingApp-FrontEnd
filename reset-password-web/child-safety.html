Umutima Child Safety & CSAE Standards

Last updated:
Externally published safety standards (CSAE)

1) Scope

Umutima is a dating app intended for adults. Users must be at least 18 years old to create an account or use the service.

2) Zero tolerance for CSAM/CSAE

Umutima has a zero-tolerance policy for child sexual abuse material (CSAM) and any form of child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE). This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Any sexual content involving a minor, or content that appears to involve a minor.
  • Solicitation, grooming, or sexual exploitation of minors.
  • Sharing, requesting, or promoting CSAM, or directing users to CSAM.
  • Any attempt to use Umutima to facilitate harm to a minor.

3) Prevention and enforcement

We use safety measures to help prevent and address CSAE/CSAM, including:

  • In-app reporting so users can report suspected child safety concerns.
  • Moderation and enforcement actions which may include content removal, account suspension, and permanent bans.
  • Fraud and abuse monitoring to detect suspicious behavior patterns.

If we identify content or behavior that violates these standards, we may take immediate action, including restricting access, disabling accounts, and preserving relevant information where necessary for safety and legal compliance.

4) Reporting to authorities

Umutima complies with applicable child safety laws and reporting obligations. When required or appropriate, we report suspected CSAM/CSAE to relevant regional or national authorities.

5) How to report child safety concerns (in-app)

If you see content or behavior that may involve a minor or CSAE/CSAM:

  • Use the Report feature inside the app (from a profile or chat).
  • Provide as much detail as possible so we can investigate quickly.

7) Additional notes

These standards are publicly available to satisfy Google Play’s child safety standards policy requirements. They apply to all Umutima users.
